Playfair Display Regular

Playfair Display was recently cultivated by Danish typeface designer Claus Eggers Sørensen in 2011. Playfair display represents transitional design, particularly signaling the style shift from broad nib quills to pointed steel pens. As printing technology advanced, letterforms were able to be designed with varying contrasts. Sorensen took inspiration from designer John Baskerville, William Martin’s ‘Boydell Shakespeare’, and ‘Scotch Roman’ designs to develop a typeface evocative of the era.
